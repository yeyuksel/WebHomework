Historical Places of Iskenderun

Column Of Yunus

Iskenderun - Payas is the remains of the entrance gate of Iskenderun on the railway spur. According to the belief of local people; The Prophet Yunus has landed here in the womb of a dolphin.

Bagras Castle

After crossing the Belen lane, a road with a 4 km bend to the right after the 27th km of Iskenderun-Antakya highway leads visitors to this magnificent pavilion. Ötençay was built on a dominant hill in a village known as Yada Buğray. There is also a church in the castle. Romans, Byzantines Eyyubis, and this panoramic view of this old village used by the Ottomans can be seen.

Alan Village

It is connected to Iskenderun by a stable road of 40 km. Haymapınar, Değirmendere, Paşaoluk, Akarca and Çamlıbel springs on this road are being used by the people of Iskenderun and surrounding villages. It is possible to camp and have a picnic in the spring, which has opened a thousand flowers in spring. 6 km after the Kırıkhan district, turn left to reach the village of Aşık Eşmişek with a road 2 km asphalt and 2 km stabilized. After the village, a plateau spreads over a very wide area covered with pine forests all around with a stabilized road surrounded by 8 km of curved and maquis plants and suddenly comes out with all its beauty. The plateau, which is completely untouched except for a few highland houses, is considered as an agricultural area by the surrounding villagers.
